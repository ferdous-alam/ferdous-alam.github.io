 <p align="justify"> 
I am Ferdous, a PhD candidate in the Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering Department at The Ohio State University and advised by <a href="https://mae.osu.edu/people/hoelzle.1">Professor David Hoelzle</a>. My general research interest is at the intersection of machine learning/reinforcement learning, control theory and optimization. For my PhD research I am developing data efficient reinforcement learning algorithms for real-world autonomous applications like **autonomous manufacturing** where collection of large dataset is not feasible. Specifically, I am using transfer learning and probabilistic strategies to make these algorithms data efficient. To demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed frameworks we have built a state-of-the-art machine that can autonomously manufacture desired artifacts with complex geometry without any human intervention. Previously we have shown that <a href="https://asmedigitalcollection.asme.org/MSEC/proceedings-abstract/MSEC2020/84263/V002T07A009/1095697">reinforcement learning can be a good candidate to tackle learning in such systems</a>. Later we developed <a href=" ">PRLAM framework that can effectively learn to manufacture 3D phononic crystals with desired properties using only 50 samples</a>. Currently I am working on the improvement and generalization of this algorithm, especially when the distribution of data is not same in the source and target tasks. We also won the best paper award at <a href="https://event.asme.org/MSEC-2020"> MSEC 2020 </a>. During my free time I enjoy writing about my favorite technical concepts e.g. RL, Probability, Control theory etc. I like to call these writings "intuitive formalism",  <a href="https://grad-student-083.netlify.app/">take a look to understand why!</a>       
</p>
