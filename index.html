I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Ohio State University where I work on machine learning, representation learning and reinforcement learning for decision making in complex real-world tasks such as design, manufacturing and robotics. I am supervised by Professor David Hoelzle. I was also a research intern and later a research collaborator at the Autodesk AI Lab where I worked with Dr. Rodger Luo on AI driven design approaches.

Research

AI/representation learning + Reinforcement learning + Control theory

⇓

Generative design + Smart Manufacturing + Autonomous Robots

My core research interest is to enable efficient decision making in complex autonomous systems. I draw from artificial intelligence (AI)/machine learning (ML), deep learning, representation learning and control theory to develop learning algorithms for various demanding tasks. My past research experience focuses on developing data-efficient learning algorithms with knowledge transfer between tasks for robot learning, representation learning and AI/ML systems integration for AI assisted design and intelligent manufacturing. I, along with my team members, have built a state-of-the-art autonomous manufacturing research robot that can take decisions in real-time to design and manufacture a complex geometry 3D artifact without any human intervention and require 10x less data samples. I have designed and implemented the software architecture for this autonomous robot. During my internship at the Autodesk AI Lab, I have worked on transformer based self-supervised approaches and autoregressive generative models for complex 3D design tasks.
